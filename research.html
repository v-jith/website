Working Papers

  • Best Arm Identification with Knapsacks: Minimax Policies

    A resource-constrained decision maker (DM) designs a continuous-time sequential experiment to determine the best choice from an array of treatments. The DM divides her attention between observing the treatments until one of the resources runs out. Under the minimax regret criterion, we characterize the optimal sampling strategy for two treatments in the following settings: (1) when there is a fixed array of resources and (2) when there is a single resource (money) and the DM can stop adaptively. Our analysis relies on a reformulation of the typical optimal stopping problem in which we model diffusions with respect to the cumulative resource expenditure rather than the elapsed time.

  • Designing Persuasive Experiments (joint with Karun Adusumilli)

    This paper is a work in progress. Click here for an abbreviated note on the work.

    We study a game of strategic interaction in which an experimenter designs a costly sequential experiment to maximize his utility subject to a welfare constraint imposed by a regulator. The experimenter chooses how to adaptively assign units across two treatments and when to stop the experiment. We characterize the optimal experiment design in a setting where outcomes are modeled by a Brownian motion and the agents share a Gaussian prior over the unknown drift of this process. We further show that this same design is asymptotically optimal in discrete time under parametric distributions of outcomes. The optimal assignment rule is the Neyman allocation after some fixed time. Furthermore, the experiment ends when the difference in posterior means exceeds one of a pair of asymmetric positive and negative thresholds that converge to 0 over time.
